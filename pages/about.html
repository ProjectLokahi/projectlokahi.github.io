Who We Are

We are a student-founded and student-run organization based on O’ahu. Coming from a diverse array of schools, families, and cultures, we all came together over our shared passion for the environment. Scroll below to meet the team!

The Founders

Kalama Abad



Kalama Abad is currently a senior at Hālau Kū Māna Public Charter school. Right before meeting with his teammates, he visited Mauna Kea where he saw how social media helped the protectors to gather an impressive group of supporters. Through Project Lōkahi, he hopes to create a platform to help facilitate similar actions to better the environment and inspire change within Hawaiʻi.

Harlow Thomas



Harlow Thomas grew up on Oʻahu, and is currently a junior at Punahou School. Growing up in Hawaii, she has seen an increase in environmental issues and plastic pollution. She is very passionate about advocating for these problems, and one day, solving them. This is why she wanted to create Project Lōkahi, a social media platform that brings together our community in a fun, and environment saving way.

Gianna Sbarbaro



Gianna Sbarbaro grew up on O’ahu and is a recent graduate of Le Jardin Academy. She has always felt very passionate about environmental issues due to her experiences in seeing environmental degradation in Hawaii. She remembers going to the beach as a child and is now saddened to see those same beaches filled with plastic. That’s why she helped create Project Lōkahi, a platform to empower students to come together and solve problems as a community.
